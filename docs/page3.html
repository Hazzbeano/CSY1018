Comparison
Software Developer (Junior)
  • £23000 - £45000 starting salary (dependent on location, experience etc..) Experienced web developers can expect their salary to rise to around £70,000 after several years in industry.
  • Java, J2EE, Spring, Hibernate, Struts, JSP, Unix/Linux, SQL, XML, HTML and C# are all examples of the paradigms that employers would like to see prospective employees be fluent in.
  • 2 Years team based experience.
  • ALL positions found that are high paying, require a degree to be eligible to apply.

Starting salaries for Software developers ranges from a reasonable amount, to a fairly large amount. This is dependent on the background of knowledge that is required e.g. python, SQL etc.. Most employers are looking for employees who work well in teams. Every position that has been found, which fall within the salary scale, require a degree to even be considered for a position.

Games Developer

Within Games Development a common expectation of employees is a strong familiarity with Unity or Unreal studio, along with a capability to work efficiently and collaboratively in a team on a tight schedule.

Starting salaries tend to be high, rarely being any lower than £40,000 per year and very few specifically ask for degree graduates.

Most jobs ask for high experience in C# / C++ programming languages (Occasionally JavaScript/Python) and a good understanding of server/client communication and the ability to design and plan solutions.

For certain jobs they may ask for experience in VR programming and 2D/3D animation.

Web Development (Junior)
  • Average starting salary of £25000, salaries can rise to £60,000+ for experienced web developers.
  • Most positions are requiring 1-2 years of industry experience.
  • Not many positions for web development require a degree to be eligible for the position.
    • CSS, HTML5, MySQL and jQuery are some of the most desirable programming languages that are required for this type of position.
  • There are a plethora of positions available for web developments, more so than any of the other 3 positions mentioned here.
